Campaign 01 · 2024–2025

Harm Reduction
is Public Health

Chicago Department of Public Health
Challenge

Chicago needed to shift public narrative around harm reduction — a politically contested approach — while building durable infrastructure for ongoing seasonal crisis response.

Approach

Built the full communications stack: visual identity for arts therapy clinics at City-run mental health facilities; billboard campaigns using City-owned advertising assets; proactive and reactive media strategy for opioid spike events including Health Alert Network (HAN) response.

Outcome

Chicago opioid overdose deaths fell (continued drop from 2023). $18M in Illinois funding secured for overdose prevention sites. National and local media coverage.

Campaign 02 · 2025

Violence Prevention,
Chicago 2025

Chicago Department of Public Health
Image
Pending

Chicago's July Fourth weekend is a ritual data point — a morbid annual benchmark that journalists, officials, and advocates all watch. In 2024, the city recorded 105 shooting victims over that weekend. In 2025: 60. Through the first half of 2025, shooting victims were running at nearly half the pace of the year prior.

These numbers don't explain themselves. Violence had been declining nationally, but Chicago's curve bent in a particular direction — and the through-line leads back to sustained investment in community violence intervention. Since 2021, over $350 million in public funding has flowed into CVI programs: street outreach workers, credible messengers, youth peacekeepers deployed to the neighborhoods where violence concentrates. The theory wasn't new. The money made it real.

My role was to help translate that investment into a story that could travel — shaping the data into narrative, and placing it with outlets like The Trace that cover this space with the seriousness it demands. The reporting didn't just reflect the trend. It traced what caused it.

Campaign 03 · 2020–2021

Centering Women Workers
During COVID-19

SEWA Bharat, India
Challenge

During COVID-19, India's informal women workers — domestic workers, textile recyclers, street vendors — were invisible in the policy conversation. Their economic and social needs were unaddressed in mainstream media.

Approach

Built a grassroots communications campaign centering workers' voices. Produced social media content and placed stories with feminist media, development sector publications, and national newspapers in India.

Outcome

Coverage in national and sector-leading publications elevated policy visibility for domestic workers and textile recyclers. Stories published in Feminism in India, Times of India, and India Development Review.

Times of India Feminism in India India Dev Review
Earlier Work · 2019

The SunPedal Ride

Clean Energy Communications · India

In the summer of 2019, 4 of us traveled along India's Golden Quadrilateral Highways on a retrofitted solar-powered electric auto-rickshaw (tuk-tuk) and a support vehicle, for 60 days and 6,000 kilometers. We spent 60 nights in different cities and towns of India, and needed all the help we could get from locals for our journey. We spent our time engaging with area locals, discussing clean energy and climate change, met policymakers and practitioners across India, eating local food, finding places of interest throughout the route, in the full blast of the Indian summer.
