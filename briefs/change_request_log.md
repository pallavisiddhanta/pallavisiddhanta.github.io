### New Narrative

Chicago's July Fourth weekend is a ritual data point — a morbid annual benchmark that journalists, officials, and advocates all watch. In 2024, the city recorded 105 shooting victims over that weekend. In 2025: 60. Through the first half of 2025, shooting victims were running at nearly half the pace of the year prior.

These numbers don't explain themselves. Violence had been declining nationally, but Chicago's curve bent in a particular direction — and the through-line leads back to sustained investment in community violence intervention. Since 2021, over $350 million in public funding has flowed into CVI programs: street outreach workers, credible messengers, youth peacekeepers deployed to the neighborhoods where violence concentrates. The theory wasn't new. The money made it real.

My role was to help translate that investment into a story that could travel — shaping the data into narrative, and placing it with outlets like The Trace that cover this space with the seriousness it demands. The reporting didn't just reflect the trend. It traced what caused it.
